how long do penguins live?

The lifespan of a penguin varies depending upon the species.
Magellanic penguins can live up to 30 years – the longest lifespan of any penguin around the world.
– while little blue penguins have the shortest lifespan of up to six years.
However, there are other factors that can influence the length of time that a penguin lives.
Penguins, like all animals, are known to live much longer in captivity as they are removed from their natural predators and have access to a reliable source of food.
Penguin chicks are also more likely to survive into adulthood as a result of the protection from external threats that captivity provides.
